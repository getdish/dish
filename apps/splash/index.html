As a millenial, most of the time, I don't give two fucks if a restaurant is dimly lit, the waiters are somewhat rude, and there's no parking.

I love discovering amazing cuisine and having a "deck" of great spots in the city. And I realized, time and time again, that searching Yelp, or even Google for "the absolute best of X", where X is a dish, was just not great. It didn't _nerd out enough_ about the data. Or if it was decent, like The Infatuated or Eater, it wasn't in a nice and easy to use app that integrated with delivery information.

This isn't a novel idea. It isn't a moonshot. What it is is a genuine attempt by a couple nerds to make something we really want to exist, something beautiful and enjoyable to use, that doens't feel like it's been designed or put together by a team of managers. It's a passion project, something we can build, think about, and geek out about for years to come.

Some sort of end note to really pull em in.
